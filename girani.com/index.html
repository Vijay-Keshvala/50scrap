    Kalpana Masala SF NYLON SABBAKKI (SABUDANA OR SAGO)

    Nylon sabudana is a type of sabudana that is steamed and dried. It is many times larger in size than regular sabudana and has a more transparent white color. Nylon sabudana is used to make sabudana chivda, which is a snack for Hindu fasting daysIn South India, regular sabudana is used to make papadam/papad called javvarisi appalam and kool vadagam. It is a crunchy and traditional accompaniment for rice dishes. Nylon sabudana is used to make sweet dishes like Javvari or sabbakki payasa/Chowwary payasam.

    Kalpana Masala SF WHITE BEANS

    White beans are a staple ingredient in cuisines across the globe. They can be boiled in soups and stews, mixed with rice or other grains, or used in casseroles. Baked beans, a popular side dish in the United States, is almost always made with white beans. Due to their high fiber and protein content, they may help support a healthy body weight, muscle mass, and digestion. White beans shine when used in refreshing salads. Their soft texture contrasts nicely with crunchy vegetables and their mild taste absorbs other flavors easily. Make a Greek salad with white beans, tomatoes, cucumbers, olives, and feta. Toss with a red wine vinaigrette. Combine white beans, arugula, roasted beets, goat cheese, and toasted walnuts. Dress with a shallot vinaigrette. Mix white beans, corn, avocado, cherry tomatoes, and red onion. Drizzle with a lime cilantro dressing.

    Horse gram split is a versatile legume commonly used in traditional cooking across various cultures. Its robust nature makes it a staple in many kitchens, valued for its rich taste and adaptability in diverse recipes.
