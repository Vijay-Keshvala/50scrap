Why Your Non-Veg Dishes Deserve Better Than Store-Bought Spice

You’ve got fresh chicken, the right cut of mutton, and time to slow-cook the dish just right… But if your masala isn’t working hard, neither is your flavour.

Most commercial non-veg masala powders are pre-ground weeks ago, packed with salt, starch, or artificial colour, and taste more like masala mix #459 than the real thing.

So what’s the difference between average and oof-that’s-good?

A fresh, balanced, punchy spice blend tailored for meat.

What Makes a Great Non-Veg Masala?

Whether it’s a chicken curry, mutton fry, or a Sunday biryani — the spice blend needs to:

  • Stand up to longer cooking and high heat
  • Blend with ghee/oil without burning
  • Compliment fat and protein-rich meats
  • Add heat, depth, and aroma — without overpowering
