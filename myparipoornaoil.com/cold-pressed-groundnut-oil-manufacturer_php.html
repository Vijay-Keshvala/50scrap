Know More About Cold Pressed Groundnut Oil

Cold pressed groundnut oil, or peanut oil, is a natural oil extracted from peanuts using traditional methods to preserve its inherent qualities. It is rich in monounsaturated fats, essential fatty acids, and vitamin E, contributing to heart health, brain health, and weight management. Its mild nutty flavor makes it suitable for various cooking methods, and it is a nutrient packed powerhouse with omega 6 and omega 9 fatty acids, vitamin E, antioxidants, and other essential nutrients.

Regular consumption of groundnut oil has been linked to improved heart health, boosts brain function, and overall well being. Its anti inflammatory properties can reduce inflammation and promote joint health. Proper storage, such as in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight, is essential for preserving its beneficial properties and extending its shelf life.

How do we Produce Healthy groundnut pressed cooking Oil?

Step 01: Sourcing and Drying

Raw groundnuts are sourced and dried for 8 to10 days to reduce moisture content.

01

Step 02: Shell Removal and Quality Check

After shell removal, peanuts are dried for 2 to 3 days, and only the highest quality nuts are selected.

02

Step 03: Cold Press Grinding

Peanuts are ground using a wooden cold pressed chekku to extract oil while preserving nutrients.

03

Step 04: Sedimentation

The extracted oil is left untouched for two days to allow sedimentation of microparticles for purity.

04

Step 05: Packaging and By products

Clear oil is packed in containers, and leftover peanut cake is used for organic farming and cattle feed.

Know About Cold Pressed Groundnut Oil Usage & Benefits

Cold pressed groundnut oil is ideal for deep frying due to its higher smoke point. However, it's crucial not to reuse oil multiple times. After frying, allow it to cool, filter, and store in dark places. Reusing oil for low heat cooking, like dosa/chapati or tadka, can extend its usage while maintaining its quality.

Cold pressed groundnut oil is a more beneficial substitute for refined sunflower oil. The oil will tolerate cooking temperatures up to 160℃. It's ideal for a range of Indian culinary styles. It is perfect for sautéing and stir frying

The standard refined groundnut oils are colorless, odorless, and tasteless. They are refined with heat and chemicals, which might ruin the oil's nutritional profile. Our cold pressed groundnut oils are produced by grinding and crushing groundnuts without external heat. It helps to preserve the natural nutrients, nutty flavor, and perfume of groundnut oil.

Cold pressed groundnut oil is high in monounsaturated fatty acids, proven to promote heart health by lowering harmful cholesterol levels and the risk of heart disease. Using cold pressed groundnut oil in your daily cooking can help improve your cardiovascular health.
