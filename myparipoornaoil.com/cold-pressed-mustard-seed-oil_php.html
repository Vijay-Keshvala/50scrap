Cold-Pressed Mustard Oil vs. Refined Mustard Oil

Cold Pressed Mustard Oil

  • Crushed at low temperatures.

  • Rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants.

  • Strong, natural, pungent flavor.

  • Longer shelf life due to natural preservation.

  • Free from harmful additives, making it a safer choice.

  • Ideal for cooking, skincare, and massages.

Refined Mustard Oil

  • Crushed at high temperatures.

  • Low in nutrients due to high heat processing.

  • Milder, sometimes neutral flavor.

  • It may have preservatives added to extend shelf life.

  • It may contain residues from chemical processing

  • Commonly used for deep frying and high-heat cooking.

How Do We Produce Healthy Cold Pressed Mustard Oil?

Step 01: Sourcing and Cleaning

High-quality mustard seeds are sourced and thoroughly cleaned to remove any impurities.

01

Step 02: Drying

The seeds are dried for a few days to reduce moisture content, which is crucial for the oil extraction process.

02

Step 03: Cold Press Grinding

The mustard seeds are ground using a wooden cold press machine (chekku) to extract the oil while preserving the natural nutrients.

03

Step 04: Sedimentation

The extracted oil is left to settle for two days to allow sedimentation of microparticles, ensuring purity.

04

Step 05: Product Packaging

The clear, sediment-free oil is packed in containers. The remaining mustard seed cake is used as cattle feed or for organic farming.
