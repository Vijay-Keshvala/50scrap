Importance of Wooden Cold Pressed Oils in Indian Food Recipes

Cold-pressed oils offer various health benefits and are suitable for specific purposes. They are rich in natural properties, making them ideal for multiple recipes. Despite variations in taste and aroma, they all share the common attribute of being chemical-free and containing the goodness of the seed or nut. Therefore, choosing the right oil based on the recipe is crucial.

Wooden cold-pressed oils like coconut, groundnut, sesame, and mustard are versatile for various recipes. Use coconut oil in curries, baking, and smoothies. Groundnut oil is used for recipes like stir-fries, deep frying, grilling, peanut butter, and traditional dishes like poha, dhokla, and parathas. Sesame oil works well in Asian dishes, salad dressings, and marinades, while mustard oil is perfect for pickles, marinades, traditional Indian dishes, and spicy chutneys. These oils also improve the flavor of dips, sauces, and sautéed vegetables.

Some Benefits of the Wooden Cold Pressed Oils

Indians have relied on traditional ghani oils, commonly known as cold pressed oils, for centuries. Often referred to as "raw and unrefined oils," these oils are gaining recognition for their superior health benefits. Emerging evidence suggests that cold pressed oils are more heat-stable, nutritious, and rich in monounsaturated fatty acids (MUFA) than their refined or industrially produced solvent oil counterparts.

Bring Home Health with Wooden Cold Pressed Oils

Cold Pressed at Below 50 Degrees

Cold pressing is a technique focused on minimizing heat during oil extraction, keeping the seeds as cool as possible. Oils are typically extracted at temperatures below 50°C, ensuring the highest quality oil with maximum retention of nutrients.

Single Pressed & Extra Pure Oil

Cold-pressed oils are typically single-pressed, resulting in extra pure and superior quality compared to refined oils. Due to harsh extraction processes, multiple-pressed oils may be cheaper but may contain unwanted by-products.

High Heat Stability in Extracted Oil

One of the important advantages of cold pressed oils is their high heat stability. These oils can withstand higher temperatures, making them suitable for various cooking methods, including deep frying, without breaking down or losing their nutritional value.

Trans Fat-Free Organic Oil

Research indicates that cold pressing does not produce harmful trans fatty acids, often found in oils extracted using high heat and chemicals.

Zero Heat Solvents Natural Oil

Cold pressed oils are extracted without using petroleum-based solvents like 'Hexane' or other toxic substances, ensuring the oil remains pure and free from harmful chemicals.

Zero Blending With Cold-Pressed Oil

High-quality cold-pressed oils are 100% single-ingredient oils, ensuring purity and authenticity, not like cheaper oils blended with cheaper ones like argemone, palm, rice bran, or soybean.

No Artificial Pungency is added to Cold Pressed Oil

Cold pressed oils do not have an artificially strong pungency because no chemicals are used to improve their aroma or flavor. Instead, they are naturally aromatic and richly flavorful, offering a more authentic taste.

Nutrition Packed in Naturally Extracted Oil

These oils are naturally packed with bioactive compounds, including vitamins, antioxidants, and healthy fats, which are easily absorbed and utilized by the body, promoting better health.

Cold Pressed Oils Used for Multipurpose

Cold pressed oils are versatile, suitable for cooking, sautéing, salads, oil pulling, and skincare. They promote better health and well-being, making them a lifestyle choice for those seeking healthier alternatives.
